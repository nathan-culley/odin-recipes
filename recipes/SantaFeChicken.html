Santa Fe Chicken

Description

Santa Fe chicken is a delicious southwestern meal that's filling, nutritious, and low-calorie. It goes well on rice, a tortilla, or just by itself. Best of all, this recipe takes advantage of the Instant Pot's incredible abilities to make this recipe quick and easy. Instead of taking hours like a slow cooker would, this recipe allows you to make Santa Fe Chicken in barely a half-hour of total prep and cooking time. This recipe was taken from 5 Dollar Dinners.

Ingredients

  • 4 small boneless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup hot water (may use chicken stock instead)
  • 15 oz can black beans
  • 15 oz can corn
  • 1 cup red salsa
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 oz cream cheese

Steps

  1. Open, drain and rinse the black beans, and open and drain the corn.
  2. Place the chicken breasts in the electric pressure cooker insert with the hot water. Pour the black beans, corn, salsa, garlic powder and ground cumin over the top.
  3. Steam valve: Sealing.
  4. Cook on: Manual/High for 15 minutes.
  5. Release: Natural or Quick.
  6. After cooking, shred the chicken with forks and stir the cream cheese into the shredded chicken and sauce. Simmer for 15 to 20 more minutes on "warm mode" to allow sauce to thicken. Stir again before serving. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Prepare veggies as side if desired.
  8. Serve Instant Pot Santa Fe Chicken in tortillas with cilantro garnish and side of veggies.
