Scrambled Eggs

Description

A simple classic enjoyed by people all over the world, scrambled eggs are regarded by many chefs as the benchmark for a good cook. Being able to cook good scrambled eggs is a textbook example of doing a simple thing well rather than a complicated thing poorly. From the simple base of a few eggs cooked in a skillet, one can add a variety of different ingredients to create a dish anyone will love.

Ingredients

  • Cooking oil (melted butter, cooking spray, olive oil, etc.)
  • 2 eggs
  • Shredded cheese (optional)
  • Sauteed vegetables (optional) (peppers and onions suggested)

Steps

  1. Put a skillet on the stove over medium heat and spread the cooking oil over the bottom of the skillet.
  2. Crack the eggs into the skillet and stir them with a spatula until the yolks have been mixed thoroughly with the whites.
  3. Let the eggs cook at medium heat, stirring gently and constantly.
  4. When the eggs start to thicken, you may add cheese and/or vegetables if desired; stir together until mixed thoroughly.
  5. When the eggs are solid, but not dried out, turn off the heat and place the eggs on a plate. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
